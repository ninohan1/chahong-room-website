# Korean Haircut Styles Guide 2025: Wolf Cut, See-Through Bangs & More

Korean haircuts have taken the world by storm. From the viral wolf cut to the delicate see-through bangs that frame every K-drama actress's face — Korean hair trends are setting the global agenda in 2025.

At CHAHONG ROOM in Myeongdong, Seoul, our stylists specialize in all the latest Korean haircut styles. Here's your complete guide to what's trending and what might suit you.

---

## 1. The Wolf Cut (울프컷)

The wolf cut is arguably the most influential haircut to emerge from Korea in recent years. Combining the volume of a shag with the texture of layers, it creates a wild, effortless look that works on all hair lengths.

**What is a wolf cut?**
A wolf cut features heavy layers at the crown for volume, with wispy, textured ends and face-framing pieces. It's inspired by 1970s rock hair but reimagined with a Korean soft aesthetic.

**Who suits a wolf cut?**
Almost everyone — the wolf cut is remarkably adaptable. It suits:
- Round, oval, square and heart face shapes
- Straight, wavy and curly hair
- Short, medium and long hair lengths
- Men and women

**Can I add a perm to my wolf cut?**
Yes — a wolf cut with a C-curl or wave perm is one of the most requested combinations at CHAHONG ROOM. The perm enhances the texture and movement of the layers.

---

## 2. See-Through Bangs (시스루뱅)

See-through bangs are the Korean answer to the traditional full fringe. Instead of a heavy, blunt bang, see-through bangs are wispy and light — you can literally see through them.

**Why are see-through bangs so popular?**
- They soften the face without overwhelming it
- They work on almost every face shape
- They grow out beautifully without an awkward in-between stage
- They pair with virtually any haircut style

**How long do they take?**
See-through bangs can be cut in 15–20 minutes. They're also available as an add-on to any haircut appointment.

---

## 3. Curtain Bangs (커튼뱅)

Curtain bangs are center-parted, face-framing bangs that fall on either side of the forehead like curtains. They've been a Korean salon staple for years and show no sign of going out of style.

**Who suits curtain bangs?**
Curtain bangs work especially well for:
- Oval and heart face shapes
- Those wanting to shorten the appearance of a long forehead
- Anyone who wants a soft, romantic look

**Can I style curtain bangs at home?**
Yes — curtain bangs are one of the easiest bang styles to style at home. A small round brush and a blow dryer creates the signature sweep effect.

---

## 4. The Mushroom Cut (머시룸컷)

The mushroom cut has exploded in popularity in Korea — particularly among men but increasingly worn by women too. It features a rounded, bowl-like shape with the sides and back tapered short.

**The Korean mushroom cut differs from traditional bowl cuts:**
- The top is fuller and more rounded
- The sides taper gradually rather than cutting bluntly
- It's often combined with a slight perm for texture
- The overall effect is chic and fashion-forward

---

## 5. The Two-Block Cut (투블럭컷) — Men's

The two-block cut is Korea's most popular men's haircut. It features short, faded sides with longer hair on top — the contrast between the two lengths creates the signature "two-block" effect.

**Variations of the two-block cut:**
- **Classic two-block** — clean, minimal, professional
- **Two-block + perm** — textured top with a wave or curl for more style
- **Two-block + fringe** — longer top styled forward over the forehead
- **Two-block undercut** — more extreme fade for a sharper look

---

## 6. The Layered Cut

Classic layered cuts remain eternally popular in Korean salons. Unlike the dramatic wolf cut, a traditional layered cut adds movement and reduces weight without changing your overall shape significantly.

**Types of Korean layered cuts:**
- **Face-framing layers** — layers that frame the face without affecting the length
- **Long layers** — subtle movement through long hair
- **Shag layers** — heavier, choppier layers for more texture
