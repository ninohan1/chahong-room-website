# Korean Perm Guide for Foreigners: Types, Prices & What to Expect in Seoul

Korean perms are nothing like the tight, frizzy perms of the 1980s. Modern Korean perm techniques create soft, natural-looking waves and curls that enhance your hair's texture without looking overdone.

If you're visiting Seoul and considering a Korean perm, this guide covers everything you need to know — including the different types, what they cost, how long they last and exactly what to expect at CHAHONG ROOM in Myeongdong.

---

## What Makes Korean Perms Different?

Korean perms focus on creating natural movement rather than uniform curls. The goal is hair that looks like it naturally waves or curls — not hair that's obviously been permed.

Key characteristics of Korean perms:

- **Soft, natural waves** rather than tight ringlets
- **Volume and movement** rather than structured curls
- **Designed for Asian hair texture** — our techniques are optimized for finer, straighter Asian hair
- **Low maintenance** — Korean perms are designed to look good even as they grow out

## Types of Korean Perms

### 1. C-Curl Perm (C컬 파마)
The most popular Korean perm. Creates soft, inward-curling ends that frame the face beautifully. The curl starts from mid-length and curves inward — like a natural blowout that never drops.

**Best for:** Medium to long hair, natural everyday look
**Duration:** 2–3 hours
**Lasts:** 4–6 months

### 2. S-Curl Perm (S컬 파마)
Creates a gentle S-shaped wave through the hair. More movement than a C-curl, less structured than a wave perm. Perfect for adding texture and volume without looking too done.

**Best for:** All hair lengths, those wanting subtle wave
**Duration:** 2–3 hours
**Lasts:** 4–6 months

### 3. Digital Perm (디지털 파마)
The most long-lasting Korean perm technique. Uses heated rods at precise temperatures to create defined waves that hold their shape even after washing. The curls look better dry than wet — opposite to traditional perms.

**Best for:** Those wanting defined, long-lasting curls
**Duration:** 3–4 hours
**Lasts:** 6–12 months

### 4. Wave Perm (웨이브 파마)
Creates loose, beachy waves throughout the hair. More relaxed and natural-looking than a digital perm, perfect for that effortless K-drama hair look.

**Best for:** Long hair, beachy natural look
**Duration:** 2–3 hours
**Lasts:** 3–5 months

### 5. Volume Perm (볼륨 파마)
Focuses on adding volume at the roots rather than curls through the length. Perfect for fine, flat hair that needs lift and body.

**Best for:** Fine hair, those wanting volume not curls
**Duration:** 2–3 hours
**Lasts:** 3–5 months

### 6. Setting Perm (셋팅 파마)
Creates structured, defined curls using a setting technique. More dramatic than other Korean perm styles — popular for special occasions and K-pop inspired looks.

**Best for:** Those wanting defined, voluminous curls
**Duration:** 3–4 hours
**Lasts:** 4–6 months

## Before Your Perm Appointment

**Do:**
- Come with clean, product-free hair
- Bring reference photos of the curl type you want
- Tell us about any previous chemical treatments (color, previous perms, keratin)
- Arrive well-rested — perm appointments can take 2–4 hours

**Don't:**
- Wash your hair within 24 hours before your appointment
- Apply heavy oils or treatments the night before
- Expect the exact same result as your reference photo — results vary based on hair type and condition

---

## After Your Perm — Aftercare Guide

The first 48 hours after your perm are crucial:

- **Do not wash your hair** for 48 hours after your perm
- **Do not tie your hair** into a ponytail or clip for 48 hours
- **Do not brush** — use your fingers to gently separate curls
- **Avoid heat** — no blow dryer on high heat for the first week

After 48 hours:
- Use a sulfate-free shampoo
- Apply curl cream or mousse while hair is damp
- Air dry when possible
- Use a diffuser attachment if blow drying

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I get a perm if my hair is colored?**
Yes, but we always assess your hair's condition first. Heavily bleached or damaged hair may not be suitable for a perm until the hair is strengthened. We'll be honest with you during consultation.

**Will a perm damage my hair?**
Modern Korean perm solutions are gentler than traditional perms. We also apply conditioning treatments before, during and after to minimize damage.

**Can men get Korean perms?**
Absolutely! Men's perms are extremely popular in Korea — the two-block perm, wave perm and volume perm are all popular among male clients.

**My hair is very straight — can I still get a perm?**
Yes — Korean perms work beautifully on straight Asian hair. In fact, straight hair often holds Korean perm results very well.

**How do I maintain my perm between salon visits?**
Use moisturizing, sulfate-free products, sleep on a silk pillowcase, and avoid excessive heat styling. Our stylists will give you personalized advice in English after your appointment.
